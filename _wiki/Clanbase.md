**Clanbase** (April 2005 - September 2005) was a first working and successful clan ladders system in LieroX community, created by [Werrock](/wiki/index.php/Werrock/).

## History

The idea of having clan ladders was brought to LieroX community by [Werrock](/wiki/index.php/Werrock/) in February 2005. At first quite many players were sceptical about having such a system but individual supporters like Kalma and [BNX](/wiki/index.php/BNX/) put a lot of effort into realisation. In March 2005 Clanbase was opened for every clan which would match the criteria to join. Clanbase was coded by Werrock and being hosted by BNX at [LieroX.net](/wiki/index.php/LieroX.net/), both were administrators. Kalma, Ruki and Anubis were moderators who could accept or reject new clans and help with Clanbase to function normally. Raziel also helped with graphics.

In April 2005 Clanbase system was drasticaly updated. More ladders were created and several important guidelines for clans were set up as well as rules regarding playing matches were set up more strictly.

It was happening that players were creating and registering fake clans just to boost up points with their primary clans. Also a lot of arguments were being brought up to Werrock regarding the game play itself. In September 2005 administrators of Clanbase were finally fed up with immature participants and Werrock has removed the Clanbase, leaving a goodbye note for several people who were helping with Clanbase.

## Rules

Leader of the clan would register his clan in Clanbase. Only one person had the position of leader in Clanbase but among clan members he could choose a trustee, which had same functions as a co-leader.

Player was not allowed to play for more than one clan in Clanbase as well as it was forbidden to register 2 different clans for individual player. Clans had to match the criteria to be accepted in Clanbase when sending the application form. Minimum ammount of members was required, clan had to exist for a certain time period and clan had to have a home page or at least a thread at Gaming Universe. Staff members were deciding if a clan would be accepted or not.

Shortly after Clanbase was opened strict rules for participants were set up. Clan wars had to consist only of 2 games with loading time between 20% and 100% being chosen by both clans. Normally each clan would pick their home settings for one match but contestants were given freedom of choice how to decide about settings. There was a list of weapons that had to be banned in each ladders and only levels in the Clanbase list could be played in a clan war. Bonuses had to be turned off. All the games were played with 10 lives as 2 vs 2 or more, duels would not count in ladders.

### Ranking system

Clanbase ranking system was based on ELO system. Changes in rating were changing according to the match results (a victory, a defeat or a draw). Like in any ELO system, how the points were given or taken after a win or a defeat depended on the rating difference between first and second opponent before the match took a place.

Without any doubt [Toilet Warriors](/wiki/index.php/Toilet_Warriors/) were the all time winners of Clanbase. ## Controversy

In April 2005 the idea to separate the Liero v1.0 ladder into Combo and Noncombo ladders was presented to the community. A poll was made at [Gaming Universe](/wiki/index.php/Gaming_Universe/) for members to vote. Idea was discriminating clans which were playing both loading times. A lot of clans played clan wars as a mixture of loading times. One game was usually being played in Rifle combo and second one in Limited 100 lt. With separated ladders this would not be possible as ladders would only allow clan wars consisting of just one setting. The strongest argument for the idea was Combos variating from Noncombos too much. Although the idea of separation has won with 26 votes against 13 at Gaming Universe, a new poll was made at Clanbase home page where only clans involved could cast a vote. Poll ended with 7 votes against 4 for splitting the ladders. One clan was banned from the Clanbase which made it a final of 6 to 4 victory for the separation. As the result 2 new ladders were created - Combo (20 lt) and Noncombo (100 lt) one. Standings in Liero v1.0 ladder as they were before splitting up were being transfered into Noncombo ladder when Combo ladder was started from scratch. A new rule regarding number of matches in clan wars was set up. Clan wars would consist only of 2 games so they could end up as a tie. Lower ranked clan would gain some points in this case and higher ranked clan would lose some. After the decision was made there was still some disagreement but as time has shown the solution turned out well.
